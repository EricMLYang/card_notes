## \[ How \]

- Before we implement our ideas, we must first unload them from our minds and give them concrete form. Only by clearing the complex thoughts from our heads can we think clearly and begin making those ideas effective in our work

- The most practical form of creativity is to connect various ideas, especially those that seem unrelated. Neuroscientist Nancy C. Andreasen conducted extensive research on highly creative individuals, including outstanding scientists, mathematicians, artists, and writers. Her conclusion was: ‘Creative people are better than others at recognizing relationships, establishing connections, and finding ways to link different concepts

- In the second brain, we can do the same thing: disrupt the order of various ideas and mix them together until unexpected ideas emerge. The more diverse and heterogeneous the materials you put into the second brain at the beginning, the more original the connections that will come out

- **CODE:** This stands for **Capture, Organize, Distill, and Express**. It's a workflow for managing information:

   - **Capture:** Effectively collect ideas and information from various sources.

      - Slack temp save, Pocket, FB Save & Share, X(twitter), kobo bookmark, LinkedLn Repost, Oreilly

   - **Organize:** Systematically store your captured information for easy retrieval.

   - **Distill:** Extract and summarize the key points of the information you've captured.

   - **Express**: Share your knowledge and insights with others.

- **PARA:** This is a system for organizing your Second Brain based on four categories:

   - Projects: Information related to specific tasks you're working on.

   - Areas: Topics of ongoing interest where you're continually learning.

   - Resources: Reference materials you might need in the future.

   - Archives: Less frequently accessed information you want to keep.
