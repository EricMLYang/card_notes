## 正文

In 1998, Warren Buffett gave a 1-hour masterclass on how to never lose money investing.

His frameworks:
- The 10% ownership test
- Castle & moat thinking
- Circle of competence
- Why smart people go broke

**12 timeless lessons from his masterclass:**

---

### 1. The 10% ownership test

Buffett asked students:

> "If you could buy 10% of one classmate for the rest of their lifetime, who would you pick?"

Not the highest IQ. Not the best grades.

You'd pick the one who's **generous, honest, and gives credit to others**.

Then he added:

> "You also have to short 10% of someone."

You'd short the egotistic, greedy, corner-cutting one.

The key:

> "Every quality on the left is achievable. Every quality on the right can be eliminated."

At your age, you choose who you become.

---

### 2. Time is the friend of the wonderful business

Time is the enemy of the mediocre business.

A wonderful business compounds. A bad one deteriorates.

The longer you hold a bad business, the worse it gets. The longer you hold a wonderful business, the better it gets.

---

### 3. The smartest people in the room went broke

Long-Term Capital Management:
- 16 people with the highest average IQ of any firm
- 350+ years of combined experience
- Hundreds of millions of their own money

They went broke.

Buffett:
> "To make money they didn't have and didn't need, they risked what they did have and did need. That is foolish."

---

### 4. You only have to get rich once

> "There was a lousy book with a great title: 'You Only Have To Get Rich Once.'"

Buffett's math:
$100 million earning 10% unleveraged vs 20% leveraged.

End of year: $110M vs $120M.

> "It makes no difference to your family. To anything."

But the leveraged path could ruin you. The 10% path never will.

---

### 5. Buy a castle with a moat

> "I want a very valuable castle. An honest Duke running it. And a big moat around it."

The moat can be:
- Low cost
- Brand power
- Share of mind
- Switching costs

The test:
> "Give me $10 billion to hurt Coca-Cola. I can't do it."

---

### 6. Circle of competence

> "I don't try to jump over 7-foot bars. I look for 1-foot bars I can step over."

Buffett doesn't try to predict every stock. He waits for what's obvious to him.

The key isn't knowing everything. It's knowing **what you know** and what you don't.

---

### 7. The institutional imperative

Institutions gravitate toward copying each other and following convention, even when it destroys value.

> "Rationality frequently wilts when the institutional imperative comes into play."

CEOs resist change. Mediocrity is self-reinforcing at scale.

---

### 8. Management quality matters more than you think

A mediocre management in a great business will eventually ruin it.

> "When a management with a reputation for brilliance tackles a business with a reputation for bad economics, it is the reputation of the business that remains intact."

---

### 9. The right kind of activity

Buffett reads 500 pages a day. Not to be busy. To be right.

> "That's how knowledge works. It builds up, like compound interest."

Information compounds. But only if you let it sit and synthesize.

---

### 10. Cola has no taste memory

> "One thing people don't understand: cola has no taste memory."

You can drink one at 9am, 11am, 3pm, 5pm. The 5pm one tastes as good as the first.

> "You can't do that with root beer or orange. Those accumulate. You get sick of them."

Result: Heavy users of Coke drink more per day than heavy users of anything else.

That's the moat.

---

### 11. The biggest mistakes are omissions

> "The biggest mistakes have not been commission. They've been omission."

Buffett's regrets:
- Healthcare stocks when Clinton's plan tanked
- Fannie Mae in the mid-80s

> "Billion-dollar mistakes that accounting doesn't pick up."

His early mistakes weren't buying bad stocks. They were **not buying great ones** when he had the chance.

---

### 12. The ovarian lottery

Buffett's thought experiment:

> "24 hours before birth, a genie says: design the world you'll live in."

You don't know your gender, nationality, family, or IQ.

You'd design a fair system — because it might be you on the wrong end.

This is how Buffett thinks about market systems and social policy. Not just morality. Game theory.

## 重點摘要

- **核心投資哲學**：不要去跳 7 英尺高的欄；尋找 1 英尺的欄就好（在能力圈內操作）
- **人格比智商重要**：如果可以買同學 10% 的股份，你選的是誠實有信用的人，不是最聰明的人
- **風險管理**：LTCM 的案例說明——用不需要的錢去冒不必要的風險是愚蠢的
- **護城河思維**：好的生意有持久的競爭優勢，時間是好生意的朋友
- **最大的錯誤是遺漏**：沒有買進的好機會比買進壞公司造成的損失更大
- **卵巢樂透**：出生前你不知道自己的起點，所以應設計一個公平的系統
