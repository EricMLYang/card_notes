# **Building a Product Roadmap as a Product Manager**

Product Development Loop

![image 29.png](./Building%20a%20Product%20Roadmap%20as%20a%20Product%20Manager-assets/image%2029.png)

## 1\. Understand

- What are the customer problems that we want to solve?

- Goal: craft a crisp statement that answer 3 question

   - What is the customer's problem

   - How do we know that this problem exists?

   - Why we must solve this problem

- Amazon: Start with the customer and work backward

   - obsessive-compulsive focus on the customer as opposed to the competitor 



### What is the customer problem

![image 30.png](./Building%20a%20Product%20Roadmap%20as%20a%20Product%20Manager-assets/image%2030.png)

![image 31.png](./Building%20a%20Product%20Roadmap%20as%20a%20Product%20Manager-assets/image%2031.png)

- Common pitfalls to avoids:

   - Product adoption is not a customer problem

      - Customers don't really care if your product metrics are declining or if people aren't using your product. Frame the problem from the customer's perspective. 

   - Start with the end customer’s problem

      - If your on team that builds product for internal stakeholders or there's other customers involved, you should really start thinking from the end customer's problem first. 

   - Writing down the problem doesn’t mean that you’re done

      - You need to do the work to understand that if there's like a real problem that exists.

### How do we know that the problem exists?

1. Talk to at least five customers about the problem

2. Dive into existing metrics and research

3. Discuss the problem with experts in your organization

4. Run an A/B test

5. Researching competitors

### Why is it critical that we solve this problem?

- How does solving this problem help your company move towards its mission and goals?

- How will you measure success?

## 2\. Identify

- What products should we build to solve the customer problems?

- Goal: Define a product that will solve the customer problem and goal that you’ve prioritized with your team.

   - Mission, vision, and strategy

   - Roadmap

   - Product requirements

## 3\. Execute

- What is the most efficient way to get the products shipped?

- Goal: Help your team build and ship products as efficiently as possible.

   - Project Management

   - Communication

   - Decision making
