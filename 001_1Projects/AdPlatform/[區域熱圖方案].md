## Multi-sensor fusion architecture balances cost, accuracy, and privacy compliance

The technical architecture must reconcile fundamentally different sensor characteristics into unified customer insights. **WiFi probe accuracy ranges 5-15 meters**, critically limited by iOS privacy restrictions (Apple blocks WiFi environment data access) and MAC randomization on iOS 14+ and Android that prevents consistent device tracking. Research indicates the MAC address space (approximately 334 billion addresses with only 39 bits allocated) makes simple hashing vulnerable to rainbow table reversal, requiring computationally expensive hash functions (SHA-256 or Argon2) combined with time-rotating peppers changed daily/weekly, device-specific salts, and truncation to 24-32 bits for k-anonymity. **BLE beacon accuracy reaches 2-3 meters with triangulation from multiple beacons**, classified into zones: immediate (<0.6-1m), near (1-8m Â±1.5m fluctuation), and far (10-40m). Detection latency runs 3-6 seconds with signals blocked by metal, water, and human bodies, requiring 2-3 beacons per station mounted 2.5+ meters high with 1-2 year battery life.

**IR thermal grids** using common hardware like AMG8833 (8Ã—8 pixels, 10 Hz, Â±0.08Â°C accuracy) or MLX90640 (32Ã—24 pixels, Â±1Â°C accuracy) provide privacy-preserving occupancy detection with >98% accuracy in normal conditions, declining with thick winter clothing. The low resolution requires bicubic interpolation, background subtraction, and blob detection post-processing, with accuracy decreasing by distance and sensitivity to heat sources like sunlight and HVAC. **Low-cost facial recognition cameras** deployed at edge achieve 99%+ accuracy with proper lighting and <5ms recognition time, storing 10,000 face profiles with fanless low-power processors. Edge computing reduces bandwidth by 7x and eliminates cloud round-trip latency, but requires higher upfront hardware costs versus cloud solutions and faces reduced accuracy compared to enterprise server-based systems.

Sensor fusion requires addressing three critical synchronization challenges. **Timestamp alignment** across heterogeneous sensors operating at different frequencies (WiFi probes at 3-second Android throttling, IR thermal at 10 Hz, beacons with 3-6 second latency, cameras at 30 fps) demands GPS/UTC hardware sync signals for millisecond accuracy or watermarking in Spark Streaming with 10-minute tolerance windows. **Spatial coordinate transformation** converts each sensor's local reference frame to a common global coordinate system through rotation matrices and translation vectors stored in sensor configuration databases. **Confidence-weighted fusion** combines positioning estimates from multiple sensors by calculating per-sensor confidence scores and weighting contributions proportionally:

## Audience profiling techniques extract actionable insights from limited sensor data

Demographic analysis from low-cost facial recognition uses **68-point facial landmark detection with deep CNN architectures** (filter sizes 32â†’512, dropout 0.5) trained on datasets like Adience and UTK Faces, achieving age estimation within Â±4.5 years and gender detection >95% accuracy in <1 second processing time. **WiFi analytics** enables anonymous longitudinal tracking through MAC address hashing with carefully designed privacy protections. The robust hashing approach applies SHA-256 to MAC + device_salt + time_pepper_part1 + time_pepper_part2, then truncates to 24-32 bits accepting <1% collision rate for k-anonymity. Time-rotating peppers changed every minute prevent cross-session tracking while maintaining same-day repeat visitor identification. This methodology captures key behavioral metrics: footfall (total unique visitors via anonymized MACs), dwell time (average in-store duration overall and by zone), visit frequency (first-time versus repeat visitor rates), and traffic patterns (peak hours, flow sequences, zone popularity). Inferring individual behavior from zone-level sensors requires sophisticated path reconstruction algorithms. The path reconstruction algorithm detects device entry through WiFi probe requests, tracks zone transitions through WiFi AP handoffs combined with beacon proximity changes, correlates with IR beam breaks for entry/exit confirmations, enriches with video path tracking for visual validation, synchronizes timestamps across all sensors, and outputs sequential zone visits with dwell times. **Repeat visitor identification** uses persistent MAC hashes as anonymous identifiers, enabling behavioral profiling across visits without knowing device owners. Hash rotation policies (daily/weekly pepper changes) limit tracking windows for privacy while building anonymous profiles capturing visit frequency (segmented as 1st visit, 2-5 visits, 5+ visits, loyal customers), preferred zones, dwell patterns, and time between visits. No linkage to personally identifiable information occurs unless customers opt in through app login or loyalty program enrollment, with all reporting aggregated at cohort level preventing individual tracking.

## Attribution models connect advertising exposure to measurable purchase outcomes

Multi-touch attribution surpasses single-touch models for retail media where customer journeys involve multiple exposures across touchpoints. **First-touch attribution** credits the initial interaction, measuring awareness and discovery campaign effectiveness but ignoring mid-funnel and bottom-funnel contributions. **Last-touch attribution** credits the final interaction before purchase, simplifying direct response measurement but over-crediting lower-funnel activities while ignoring the customer journey buildup. **Multi-touch linear attribution** distributes credit equally across all touchpoints, while **time-decay attribution** weights recent interactions more heavily. **Position-based (U-shaped) attribution** allocates 40% to first touch, 40% to last touch, and 20% distributed across middle touches, recognizing that awareness and conversion moments carry special significance. **Data-driven algorithmic attribution** applies machine learning to determine credit distribution based on actual conversion patterns, requiring substantial data volume but delivering most accurate insights.

The convenience store environment enables uniquely powerful **closed-loop attribution** linking ad exposure â†’ product consideration â†’ purchase within minutes or hours rather than days or weeks. Implementation follows five stages: (1) Campaign activation embeds tracking parameters (campaign ID, content variant) in digital signage content, (2) Exposure tracking uses computer vision to log Opportunity to See (OTS) and attention metrics while WiFi/beacons log presence and zone entry with timestamp + anonymous ID, (3) Behavioral tracking captures zone dwell times and path to product with interaction logging where shelf sensors exist, (4) Transaction capture records POS purchases with time/location matched to exposure data for probabilistic attribution, (5) Analysis and optimization calculates campaign performance (ROAS, lift, incrementality) and creative effectiveness for budget reallocation.

**Sales lift analysis** quantifies incremental impact: Sales Lift % = (Sales During Campaign - Baseline Sales) / Baseline Sales Ã— 100, then Attributed Revenue = Sales Lift Ã— Average Transaction Value, finally ROAS = Attributed Revenue / Ad Spend. Industry benchmarks show **13-17% sales lift** typical for in-store digital campaigns, with a battery brand case study achieving 13.5% lift and 10% transaction growth over four weeks. DOOH gas station campaign delivered 17% lift with 46% new-to-brand customers and $5.37 ROAS. Convenience store dwell time of 3-7 minutes combined with checkout captive audience of 2-5 minutes provides high-quality exposure windows, with research showing 51% who saw DOOH with directions visited the business and 93% of visitors purchased.

**Incrementality testing** establishes causation rather than correlation through controlled experiments. Geo-based holdout compares test markets (campaign runs) versus control markets (no campaign/holdout) with sales difference representing incremental lift, requiring demographically and historically similar markets. Audience-based control randomly assigns 50% exposed and 50% suppressed within same market, though preventing contamination between groups challenges implementation. Time-based experiments alternate on/off periods requiring careful seasonality controls. Synthetic controls use machine learning to create virtual control groups with characteristics matching exposed groups, enabling post-campaign comparison of actual performance versus predicted no-ad scenario. Best practices require minimum 14-day test duration accounting for purchase cycles, 95% statistical confidence thresholds, upfront sample size calculations, and controls for external factors including holidays, promotions, and weather.

**POS integration** closes the attribution loop through three architectural patterns. Real-time API integration flows transaction data from POS system to analytics platform via REST APIs enabling live dashboards and immediate attribution. Batch processing exports end-of-day POS data through ETL pipelines suitable for historical analysis and overnight reporting. Cloud data warehousing (Snowflake, BigQuery) combines POS data with WiFi analytics, ad exposure, and customer profiles for advanced analytics and ML models. Anonymous linking without PII uses time-window attribution (WiFi MAC hash detected within same time window as POS transaction creates probabilistic match if only one transaction occurred), basket composition matching (digital signage shows specific product, POS basket contains that product plus complementary items matching signature basket pattern), zone-based attribution (device path through zones to advertised product correlates with POS scan within minutes), and aggregated store-level analysis (total ad impressions versus total sales of advertised item with lift measurement versus non-advertised periods or control stores).

## Taiwan PDPA compliance requires differential privacy and explicit consent protocols

Taiwan's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA), amended May 31, 2023, classifies **facial recognition data as sensitive personal data** (physical characteristics) requiring one of four legal grounds: expressly required by law, necessary for public interest, explicit consent from data subject, or data already made public by subject. For retail analytics, explicit consent represents the primary viable path, requiring informing data subjects of purpose, scope, usage impact, and data subject rights (access, deletion, objection) with separate consent needed for uses beyond original purpose. Penalties range NT$2M-15M for security breaches, up to NT$200K for marketing violations, enforced by the Preparatory Office of Personal Data Protection Commission with extraterritorial reach applying to entities outside Taiwan collecting data of Taiwan nationals.

**Simple MAC address hashing fails PDPA anonymization standards** because the MAC address space of only \~334 billion addresses (39 bits allocated, 50% enumerable in 31 bits) enables rainbow table reversal, as explicitly warned by the FTC. The robust PDPA-compliant approach implements six layers: (1) secret pepper split between sensor and central server, (2) time-rotating peppers changed daily/weekly preventing long-term tracking, (3) salted hashing with per-device random salt, (4) computationally expensive hash function (SHA-256 minimum, Argon2 or PBKDF2 preferred), (5) truncation to 24-32 bits for k-anonymity accepting <1% collision rate, (6) immediate processing with original MAC address discarded before storage. Formula: HASH(MAC + device_salt + time_pepper_part1 + time_pepper_part2) â†’ Truncate to 24-32 bits.

**Differential privacy** represents the gold standard for anonymization, satisfying GDPR, CCPA, and PDPA requirements through mathematical guarantees. The framework adds calibrated noise to data or query results controlled by privacy budget epsilon (Îµ), with typical values Îµ = 0.1-1.0 for strong privacy (lower Îµ = stronger privacy but less accuracy). Key properties include withstanding linkage, differential, and reconstruction attacks; compositional privacy enabling calculation of cumulative privacy loss; and impossibility of determining if specific individual exists in dataset. Adopted by Google, Apple, U.S. Census, Snowflake, and BigQuery, differential privacy applications in retail include Customer 360 analytics, cross-outlet behavior analysis, supply chain benchmarking, and aggregated purchase insights.

**Edge processing for video analytics** provides the most privacy-preserving architecture by processing facial recognition and analytics on camera devices, extracting only needed metrics (count, dwell time, demographics) while never transmitting or storing raw images. Under PDPA interpretation, if output remains non-identifiable, it may not constitute "personal data" triggering regulatory requirements. Implementation deploys AI models on edge cameras outputting aggregate counts, average age ranges, and gender distributions while deleting processed frames immediately, transmitting only anonymized statistics to cloud systems. Data minimization, a core PDPA principle requiring processing not exceed necessary scope of specific purposes, mandates collecting only essential data points, immediate deletion of unnecessary fields, storing aggregated insights rather than raw data, and time-limited retention (24-72 hours for granular data).

Compliance checklist for deployment includes pre-deployment requirements (legal ground established, specific purpose documented, data minimization plan, consent mechanism designed, privacy notice prepared, security measures implemented, vendor PDPA compliance verified, retention policy defined, cross-border transfer reviewed), facial recognition specific requirements (sensitive data legal ground documented, edge processing implemented, anonymization at source with no raw image storage, explicit consent obtained, opt-out mechanism provided, children's data protection ensured), WiFi tracking requirements (robust MAC hashing with salted, peppered, time-rotating keys, hash before storage never storing raw MAC, time-limited tracking, aggregation only with no individual patterns, signage posted, opt-out method available), and operational requirements (transparency notices posted in-store, staff trained on data handling, data subject rights procedures for access/deletion/objection, breach response plan with 72-hour notification capability, activity logs maintained, regular compliance audits, vendor contracts including PDPA obligations).
